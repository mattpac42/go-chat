# Scaling Up: Execution Habits Summary

## Overview

The 10 Rockefeller Habits form the execution backbone of the Scaling Up methodology, originally developed by John D. Rockefeller and modernized by Verne Harnish. These habits create rhythm, accountability, and alignment across growing organizations.

## The 10 Rockefeller Habits

### 1. Executive Team is Healthy and Aligned
- Leadership team demonstrates cohesion and trust
- Five dysfunctions of teams framework (Patrick Lencioni) addressed
- No tolerance for triangulation or backdoor conversations
- Regular team health assessments

### 2. Everyone is Aligned with the #1 Thing
- Quarterly theme drives organizational focus
- "Keep the main thing the main thing" philosophy
- All activities ladder up to top priority
- Example: Mark Zuckerberg's "Get Mobile" theme aligned entire Facebook organization

### 3. Communication Rhythm is Established
- **Daily Huddles**: 5-15 minutes, same time/place, standing preferred
- **Weekly Meetings**: 60-90 minutes, tactical issue resolution
- **Monthly Meetings**: Half-day strategic reviews
- **Quarterly Planning**: Full-day or multi-day strategic sessions
- **Annual Planning**: 2-3 day comprehensive strategic planning

### 4. Customer and Employee Feedback Collected
- Net Promoter Score (NPS) tracking
- Regular pulse surveys
- Exit interviews analyzed for patterns
- Customer advisory boards
- Real-time feedback loops

### 5. Peer-to-Peer Accountability System
- Who-What-When (WWW) commitments public
- Team members hold each other accountable
- No reliance on top-down enforcement
- Scorecards visible to all team members

### 6. Core Values and Purpose are "Alive" in Organization
- Values referenced in every meeting
- Hiring/firing decisions based on values alignment
- Recognition programs tied to values
- Purpose drives strategy discussions

### 7. Employees Can Articulate Strategy
- One-page strategic plan accessible to all
- Every employee knows: Core Customer, Brand Promise, Key Initiatives
- Regular town halls reinforce strategic direction
- Visual management makes strategy visible

### 8. All Team Members Use a One-Page Plan
- Personal accountability documents
- Quarterly priorities aligned with company goals
- Individual metrics tracked
- 90-day rhythm for review and reset

### 9. Core Processes Run Smoothly
- Critical processes documented
- Process owners assigned
- Regular process audits
- Continuous improvement culture

### 10. KPIs and Plan Reviewed Weekly/Monthly
- Weekly metric reviews in leadership meetings
- Monthly deep-dives into lagging indicators
- Red/yellow/green dashboards
- Leading indicators predict future performance

## Meeting Rhythms: The Heartbeat of Execution

### Daily Huddles (5-15 minutes)
**Purpose**: Start the day aligned with priorities and obstacles visible

**T. Boone Pickens Example**: Oil tycoon Pickens held daily 7:30 AM huddles for 40+ years. Same time, same place, standing room only. Each person shared their daily priority and any blockers. Meeting never exceeded 15 minutes but created unstoppable momentum.

**Format**:
- What's up? (good news, wins from yesterday)
- Daily metrics (2-3 key numbers)
- Where are you stuck? (blockers only)
- Daily priorities (one thing per person)

**Rules**:
- Start/end on time
- Stand up (keeps it short)
- No problem-solving (take offline)
- Public commitments

### Weekly Meetings (60-90 minutes)
**Purpose**: Tactical issue resolution and weekly metric review

**Agenda**:
- Good news (5 min)
- Scorecard review (10 min)
- Rock/priority updates (15 min)
- Customer/employee feedback (10 min)
- Issue identification and prioritization (10 min)
- Issue resolution (30-40 min)
- Cascade messaging (5 min)
- Rating (1-10, quick feedback)

**Key Practice**: Who-What-When format for all commitments
- **Who**: Single point person (no shared accountability)
- **What**: Specific deliverable (not activity)
- **When**: Concrete deadline (date + time)

### Monthly Meetings (Half-day)
**Purpose**: Strategic review, deeper issue resolution, learning

**Mom's Organic Market Example**: CEO Scott Nash runs monthly leadership meetings that include:
- Financial deep-dive (trends, variances)
- Strategic initiative updates
- One educational topic (book, article, case study)
- Longer-term issues requiring strategic thinking

### Quarterly Planning (Full-day)
**Purpose**: Set 13-week sprint priorities, adjust annual plan

**Components**:
- Previous quarter review
- Market/competitive intelligence
- SWOT update
- Quarterly theme selection
- 3-5 company priorities (rocks)
- Departmental priorities aligned to company rocks
- Key initiatives and resources

### Annual Planning (2-3 days)
**Purpose**: Set strategic direction, cascade throughout organization

**Best Practice**: Offsite location, outside facilitator, pre-work completed

## Implementation Roadmap

### Months 1-3: Foundation
1. Establish daily huddles first (highest ROI)
2. Create simple scorecards (5-10 metrics)
3. Implement Who-What-When tracking
4. Start weekly meetings with basic agenda

### Months 4-6: Depth
1. Add monthly meetings
2. Develop quarterly planning rhythm
3. Cascade meetings to all levels
4. Refine metrics and dashboards

### Months 7-12: Mastery
1. Full annual planning process
2. Peer accountability systems mature
3. All 10 habits operational
4. Continuous improvement focus

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Meetings drift to problem-solving**: Use IDS (Identify, Discuss, Solve) framework
2. **Lack of preparation**: Pre-work required for all meetings
3. **Too many metrics**: Start with 5-10, add carefully
4. **Cascading failure**: Must cascade from top to all levels
5. **Inconsistency**: Rhythm breaks down = results break down

## Key Metrics for Habit Health

- Daily huddle attendance (target: 95%+)
- Who-What-When completion rate (target: 90%+)
- Weekly meeting effectiveness rating (target: 8+/10)
- Scorecard updates on-time (target: 100%)
- Quarterly priorities achieved (target: 80%+)

## Bottom Line

The Rockefeller Habits create organizational rhythm that transforms chaotic growth into predictable scaling. Companies that master these habits report:
- 3-5x faster decision-making
- 40-60% reduction in meetings
- 90%+ strategy execution rates
- Significantly improved employee engagement

**Start small**: Daily huddles and weekly meetings. Master these before adding complexity. Rhythm beats perfection every time.
